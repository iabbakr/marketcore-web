Dealers Guideline

Everything you need to know about joining, using, and getting the most out of the Marketcore dealer platform. Read this before you register.

1. Overview

Marketcore is Nigeria's trusted phone verification and dealer management platform. The mobile application is built exclusively for verified electronics dealers — giving you tools to manage device transactions, build your professional reputation, connect with verified peers, and protect your business.

The web platform (this site) is open to all Nigerians — anyone can check a phone's status or report a stolen device for a small one-time fee. Becoming a dealer gives you access to the full platform at no monthly cost.

Web is for everyone. Mobile is for dealers. As a registered dealer, you use the Marketcore mobile app (iOS and Android) for all dealer-specific features. The web platform handles public checks and reports.

2. Eligibility

To register as a dealer on Marketcore, you must meet all of the following criteria:

  • Be at least 18 years of age
  • Be a Nigerian citizen or legal resident
  • Operate a legitimate electronics dealing business (new, used, or repair)
  • Possess a valid NIN (National Identification Number)
  • Possess a valid BVN (Bank Verification Number)
  • Have a valid email address and Nigerian phone number
  • Not be previously banned from the Marketcore platform

If your NIN or BVN appears in our banned identity registry, your registration will be automatically rejected. Banned users cannot create new accounts using the same identity documents.

Your NIN and BVN are encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption and are never shared with third parties except for verification purposes. They are used exclusively to:

  • Verify your identity and prevent duplicate accounts
  • Check against the banned identity registry
  • Ensure compliance with Nigerian financial regulations

See our Privacy Policy for complete details on data handling.

5. Transactions — Assign Phone Feature

The Assign Phone feature allows one dealer to assign a device to another dealer (collector) for sale, with all terms tracked on the platform. This creates a legally traceable agreement between both parties.

How It Works

  • Owner assigns a phone to a Collector at an agreed price
  • Both parties receive a notification and digital record of the assignment
  • The owner can update the transaction status: Paid, Returned, or Theft Reported
  • All transaction records are permanently logged for legal reference

Transaction Statuses

  • Assigned — Device is with the collector, payment pending
  • Paid — Collector has remitted payment to the owner
  • Returned — Device was returned to the owner unsold
  • Theft Reported — Owner has escalated to theft status (see below)

⚖️ Legal Agreement — Device Collection

By assigning or accepting a phone assignment on Marketcore, both parties enter into a legally binding agreement under Nigerian law. Specifically:

Any dealer who collects a device from another dealer's shop and fails to return the device or remit payment within the agreed timeframe shall be liable to pay 150% of the assigned device value as damages, in addition to the original price.

This is enforceable under the Sale of Goods Act (Sections 45–52, LFN 2004) and the Nigerian Contract Law. Marketcore transaction records serve as valid evidence in legal proceedings.

Reporting a transaction as Theft triggers an alert to all parties and generates a legal reference that the owner can present to the Nigerian Police Force (NPF). False theft reports are a criminal offence.

6. Device Checker (Dealer Access)

As a verified dealer, you can check any device's status within the mobile app as part of your standard account features. The public web checker costs ₦100 per check — dealer mobile app access is included with your account.

Always check a device before:

  • Purchasing a used device for resale
  • Accepting a device as collateral or on consignment
  • Transacting with an unfamiliar seller

A clean result means the device is not in our database — it does not guarantee the phone was never stolen. Always request a purchase receipt and physically inspect the device.

7. Dealer Groups

Dealer groups are location-based communities within the Marketcore app. Each group is scoped to a geographic level:

  • All Nigeria — Open to all verified dealers nationwide
  • State groups — Open to dealers in a specific state (e.g. Lagos, Rivers, Kano)
  • City groups — Open to dealers in a specific city (e.g. Computer Village Ikeja)
  • Area groups — Hyper-local to a specific market area

Within groups, dealers can share products, post updates, ask questions, and message each other directly. Group posts support images and text. Inappropriate content will result in post removal and account review.

8. Ratings System

After completing a transaction, dealers rate each other on a 1–5 star scale. Ratings are tied to specific transaction IDs — you cannot rate a dealer without a completed transaction with them.

  • Ratings are permanent and cannot be deleted by the rated dealer
  • You can only rate a dealer once per transaction
  • Ratings may include an optional written comment
  • Abusive or false ratings can be disputed by contacting dealers@marketcore.ng

9. Code of Conduct

All Marketcore dealers are expected to maintain the highest standards of professional conduct. The following are strictly prohibited:

  • Buying, selling, or handling stolen devices
  • Submitting false or fraudulent KYC information
  • Submitting false device reports or theft allegations
  • Harassing, threatening, or abusing other dealers or users
  • Creating multiple accounts to circumvent a suspension or ban
  • Sharing your account credentials with any other person
  • Misusing the transaction system (fake assignments, collusion)
  • Posting inappropriate, offensive, or misleading content in groups

Violation of any code of conduct rule may result in immediate suspension or permanent ban without a refund of any fees paid. Serious violations will be reported to Nigerian law enforcement.

10. Suspension & Bans

Account Suspension

An account may be suspended temporarily for:

  • Receiving 3 or more bad ratings (1–2 stars)
  • A pending investigation by our admin team
  • Suspected fraudulent activity pending review

During suspension, you cannot assign phones, join groups, or send messages. You may still log in and view your account. Suspended accounts are reviewed within 5 business days.

Permanent Ban

Permanent bans are issued for:

  • Confirmed fraud, theft, or criminal activity
  • Submitting deliberately false KYC information
  • Repeated code of conduct violations after warnings
  • Court orders requiring account termination

Upon permanent ban, your NIN and BVN are added to the Marketcore banned identity registry. No new accounts may be created using those identity documents.

Pardons

Suspended (not banned) dealers may be pardoned by an admin after investigation. A pardon resets your bad rating count to zero and restores full account access. Permanently banned accounts cannot be pardoned except by a valid court order.
